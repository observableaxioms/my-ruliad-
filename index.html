My Ruliad

Mike Ross from Suits said something that stuck with me:

"I like to read, once I read something, I understand it, and once I understand it, I never forget it."

As a kid who was left on my own ample time, I taught myself everything from reading.

I voraciously read encyclopedias at age 10 & thought a lot about quantum principles, stumbling upon the idea of the Ruliad & Hyperuliad.

I thought our ideas of god could have derived from aliens with alien intelligence who came to earth at a prehistoric time, and could access dimensions we cannot see from our fishbowl.

Later, I came to meet someone who was exactly like me. Although he was born on the other side of the world, he had a genuine love for science and experimentation out of pure curiosity like I did.

I feel like, finally for the first time in my life, my emes have arranged themselves in a way that quiets my brain, like when I am swimming or reading.

My whole life - the reason why I was into black holes, Einstein, math, it all makes sense. What I thought was sci-fi was actually just the concept of the Ruliad & Hyperuliad!

Physics & math only seem different to us because as observers within the ruliad, we cannot sample outside our rulial space.

As an endlessly curious person, metamathematics makes me see the world in the way I felt like I always have seen it, like how natural it felt when I did freestyle strokes back and forth after school to deal with my nerves from college applications.
